# Volunteer Rostering — a proposal

*Clonbur Woods parkrun · for the organising group*

## The idea

Putting the weekly roster together is fiddly and time-consuming, and the job rotates between us. We’re proposing to host a simple web based tool replacing the What’s app poll, also assisting the coordinator to make and adjust a draft roster before entering it in EMS normally.

Each month, volunteers fill in a short poll saying which Saturdays they can help and any jobs they’d like. The tool then produces a **draft roster** for each week that follows our usual rules, which the week’s coordinator can adjust and enter into parkrun. It’s meant to do the tedious part — working out who does what — while a person stays in charge of the final say.

**What it would make easier:**

* **No more detective work or unidentified volunteers.** Today the poll gives us first names (“Ger”, “Marie”) and we have to work out who each person actually is to find them in the parkrun system. The new poll would ask each unidentified volunteer for their **parkrun barcode** once, so everyone is identified correctly from the start. (Initially the volunteer pool can be “seeded” by importing saved pages from EMS)

* **Fair rotation.** The tool spreads jobs around so people get variety week to week.

* **Less work for whoever’s on roster duty**, and a consistent result no matter who’s doing it.

* **A clearer footing on data protection.** The current WhatsApp poll shows everyone's phone number to the whole group and has no privacy notice or control over how the information is kept. (This is What’s App’s problem not ours of course).  A dedicated tool can ask for only what's needed (name and barcode), explain clearly how it's used, keep it securely, and let people be removed on request.

**What it would *not* do:** it doesn’t plug into or change the parkrun system. It only prepares a draft; the coordinator still reviews it and enters the roster into parkrun as they do now.

**Possible drawback**: we can’t see in What’s app how many people have responded (although this could be shown in the tool).

## How it would work

**For volunteers (once a month):**

1. Open the poll link.
2. Pick your name from the list — or, if you’re new, enter your parkrun barcode once.
3. Tick the Saturdays you can help, and (optionally) request a job you’d like.

**For the coordinator (each week):**

1. Click **Generate** to get a draft roster.
2. Adjust anything you want.
3. Enter it into EMS.
4. Screengrab and post in the What’s app group as before.

## The rules, as we’ve understood them 

This is our interpretation of how the roster works. **T**he tool follows these rules.

**Nine jobs to fill every week:** Run Director, Course Check, First Timers Welcome, two Timekeepers, Barcode Scanning, Finish Tokens, Marshal, Tail Walker.

**Handled separately (not part of the poll):** Results Processor (usually Shauna or Yvonne) and Volunteer Co-ordinator (whoever is running the roster that week).

**Minimum of 7 people** each week. We get from nine jobs down to seven people by letting some people cover two jobs, using the usual combinations:

* Run Director **also** does Finish Tokens.

* First Timers Welcome is **also** done by whoever’s on Course Check, Barcode Scanning, or a Timekeeper.

No other combinations are possible — a person can’t be in two places during the run. If there aren’t enough people to cover the nine jobs using those pairings, the tool leaves roles **unfilled** and tells you that you need more volunteers, rather than inventing an impossible roster.

**Other rules:**

* Nobody does more than **two** jobs.

* **Run Director** goes to someone who has done it before or manually chosen.

* We **honour people’s job requests first**, then fill the remaining jobs so that jobs get spread around for variety.


## Does it actually work? Two real examples

We tested it on two real weeks using the actual poll responses.

### Saturday 4 July

We gave it that week’s poll and who was available, and compared its draft to the roster we actually ran on the day. It produced a valid roster, **honoured everyone’s job requests**, flagged no problems, and **5 of the 9 jobs matched exactly** what we really did — including **Eilís covering both Run Director and Finish Tokens**, just as happened on the day. Its draft:

| Job | Suggested |
| :---- | :---- |
| Run Director | Eilís |
| Finish Tokens | Eilís *(doubling up — an allowed combination)* |
| Course Check | Yvonne *(had requested this)* |
| First Timers Welcome | Edel |
| Timekeeper | Edel |
| Timekeeper | Olivia *(had requested this)* |
| Barcode Scanning | Bláithín |
| Marshal | Geraldine |
| Tail Walker | Ann *(had requested Marshal or Tail Walker)* |

Importantly, **two of the seven people who voted couldn't be identified from their names**, so they couldn't be included in the draft at all — a real example of the problem the barcode step is designed to fix. The remaining differences from what we actually ran were just interchangeable choices (who marshals vs. tail-walks), plus one person we’d used that day (Shauna) who hadn't voted in this poll.

### Saturday 11 July

Only **five** of the seven people who voted could be identified from their names — so the tool correctly flagged that the week was **below the 7-person minimum** and effectively told us *“you need more volunteers this week”*, rather than quietly producing an over-stretched roster. That’s the behaviour we’d want.

## The one thing we’d need from the group

To build a roster, the tool needs to know exactly who each volunteer is, and the only reliable way to do that is the **parkrun barcode** (names aren’t unique, and we can’t always tell who’s who). In both sample weeks, **two or three of every seven** poll responses couldn’t be matched to a person from the name alone.

So the new poll would ask each volunteer for their barcode **once**. We’d store only names and barcodes, kept privately for rostering. Getting everyone’s barcode in is the single thing that makes the whole idea work.
